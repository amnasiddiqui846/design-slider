ELEPHANT
Elephants are highly intelligent and deeply social animals with strong family bonds. They live in matriarch-led herds and show empathy, memory, and cooperative care. Despite their size, they are generally gentle unless threatened. They communicate using vibrations, sounds, and body language. Elephants are protective, especially of calves. Their nature is calm, emotional, and wise

WHITW HORSE
Horses are herd animals with sensitive and alert personalities. White horses often symbolize purity, grace, and nobility in many cultures. They are fast, responsive, and rely heavily on trust and awareness. Horses can be both powerful and gentle depending on handling. Their instincts prioritize safety and group movement. Their nature blends elegance with strength.

WHITE FOX
Arctic foxes are intelligent, adaptable, and quiet hunters. They survive harsh climates using thick fur and opportunistic feeding habits. Usually solitary or in small family groups, they rely on stealth. They are curious but cautious around threats. Arctic foxes store food and adapt quickly to changing conditions. Their nature is clever, resilient, and observant.

LION
Lions are social predators that live in prides, unlike most other big cats. They are territorial, confident, and often display calm dominance rather than constant aggression, Males typically guard territory while females handle most hunting., Their behavior balances laziness with explosive bursts of power., Lions are opportunistic hunters and prefer teamwork. Their nature reflects authority, patience, and controlled strength.

MARKHOR
Markhors are shy, sure-footed mountain animals adapted to steep terrain. They prefer high altitudes and rugged landscapes for safety. Males are mostly solitary while females form small groups. They are cautious and rely on agility to escape predators. Markhors are strong climbers and extremely resilient. Their nature is wild, defensive, and independent.
